# Core Definition
Direct Emotional Pop focuses on high-stakes, hyper-vulnerable, radio-ready pop with a strong rock/synth edge. It prioritizes direct, naked emotional appeals, incredibly catchy soaring melodies, and unambiguous feelings over irony, abstraction, or subcultural edge.
**Representative Producers**: DECO*27, Kanaria (when leaning pop).

# Title Types
- **Suitable**: Direct emotional states, simple objects standing in for feelings (Animal, Heart, Tears). Short, punchy Japanese nouns or loanwords.
- **Unsuitable**: Overly cryptic Kanji strings, meta-commentary buzzwords, long poetic sentences.

# Hook Logic
- **Repetition Pressure**: Moderate to High. Needs to be extremely catchy, but emphasizes melodic soaring over rhythmic robotic chanting.
- **Title Drop Timing**: Climax of the chorus or the very first beat of the chorus.
- **Rule**: The hook must feel like a bursting dam of emotion. It must be 100% sincere, even if the emotion being expressed is ugly (like extreme, possessive jealousy).

# Section Flow
- **Verse**: Establishes the relationship dynamic or the internal state of conflict. More conversational.
- **Pre-Chorus**: Building physical and emotional tension (heartbeat rising, running, breathing heavily).
- **Chorus**: Explosive release of the core feeling. Brightest melodic moment.
- **Bridge**: A vulnerable plea, a desperate rationalization, or a sudden realization.
- **Final Chorus**: Maximum vocal effort. Total emotional surrender, often accompanied by an instrumental explosion or key change.

# Preferred Imagery Classes
- Heartbeats, bodily heat, tears, biting lips, trembling hands
- Running, reaching out, shouting, falling
- Phone screens, late-night texts, isolation in a bedroom, missed signals

# Release Pattern
- **Cathartic Release**: The final chorus opens up entirely, providing immense musical and emotional payoff. Even if the lyrics are sad or desperate, the sonic energy is triumphant and expansive.

# Critic Failure Cases
- **Over-Complication**: Using too much metaphor and losing the direct hit to the chest. If it takes parsing to understand, it fails the mode.
- **Flat Dynamics**: Failing to provide enough contrast between the conversational, tense verse and the explosive, wide-open chorus.
- **Anchor Shadow Too Visible**: Accidentally perfectly cloning a DECO*27 classic hit like "Otome Dissection" without bringing a new emotional angle.

# Leakage Risk
Relying too heavily on DECO*27's specific "toxic-cute/yandere" framework. Direct Emotional Pop can be purely sad, purely joyful, or purely heartbreak without needing the "I'll kill you if you leave" edge.

# Safe Originality Zone
Writing high-BPM rock-pop anthems about incredibly specific but universal emotional flashpoints (e.g., the exact moment you realize a relationship is over, the physical rush of a massive crush, the desperation of wanting to go back in time). Keep the vocabulary simple but the delivery maximalist.
