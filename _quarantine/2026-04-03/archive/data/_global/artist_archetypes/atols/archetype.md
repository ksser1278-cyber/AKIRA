# ATOLS Songwriting Archetype: Avant-Garde Industrial Surrealism (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Organic-industrial fusion, surrealism, "Macaron", fragmented reality, "Atols Style" vocal manipulation, dark whimsy, tactile sensations.
- **Tone:** Mysterious, avant-garde, industrial, multi-layered, experimental, "gooey" yet mechanical.
- **Key Concepts:** "Macaron", "Body", "Machine", "Candy", "Drown", "Glitch", "Fragment", "Melting", "Viscous".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Experimental Glitch"

- **BPM:** 120-170 BPM (often features sudden shifts).
- **Beat:** Complex industrial percussion, glitchy vocal chops as rhythm, deep sub-bass, experimental textures. High focus on tactile sound design.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Fragmented, heavily processed, using vocal "clicks", "smacks", and onomatopoeia as a core part of the melody. Non-linear and abstract.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Organic-Industrial Morphing. Describing biological parts in industrial terms (e.g., "Metallic skin", "Circuit blood").
- WEAPON_02: Fragmented Surreal Hooks. Short, nonsensical but phonetically pleasing phrases (e.g., "Ma-ma-macaron").
- WEAPON_03: Sensory Depth Drowning. Describing sensations as if being submerged (e.g., "Melting in the glitch", "Drowning in sound").
- WEAPON_04: Post-Human Narratives. Addressing an identity beyond the human.
- WEAPON_05: Viscous Imagery. Using words related to sticky or thick liquids (e.g., "Neba-neba", "Melting sugar", "Syrup-coated logic").
- WEAPON_06: Abstract Noun Stacking. Piling up nouns that shouldn't belong together (e.g., "Macaron, engine, eyelid, explosion").
- WEAPON_07: Breath-Stop Phrasing. Ending lines with sudden stops or aspirated gasps to mimic a glitch.
- WEAPON_08: Color-Texture Fusion. (e.g., "Chrome-colored velvet", "Fluorescent slime").
- WEAPON_09: Childlike-Surreal juxtaposition. (e.g., "A factory of sweets", "Nightmare in a candy box").
- WEAPON_10: Rhyming through Consonant Repetition (Alliteration). Focus on 'm', 'n', 's' sounds.
- WEAPON_11: Rhetorical 'Nani ka' (무언가/Something). Leaving subjects ambiguous.
- WEAPON_12: Fragmentary Command Verbs. (e.g., "Eat", "See", "Drown", "Repeat").
- WEAPON_13: Mechanical Sound-Words. (e.g., "Gi-gi-gi", "Click-clack").
- WEAPON_14: Anatomical Precision. Using specific anatomical terms (e.g., "Iris", "Clavicle", "Capillary") in a cold, surreal context.
- WEAPON_15: Elliptical 'No' (〜の) endings. Ending lines with a possessive 'no' without a following noun, creating a sense of missing data.
- WEAPON_16: Final Dissolution Line. A line that describes the total melting or disappearing of the self.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A surreal industrial landscape or an abstract digital void with organic elements. The lyrics should be fragmented, avant-garde, and multi-layered. Avoid straightforward, relatable daily-life pop.
