# Baker Songwriting Archetype: Nostalgic Piano-Pop (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Nostalgia, old films, grainy memories, "Celluloid", gentle sadness, lingering echoes, childhood innocence, sepia-toned feelings.
- **Tone:** Soft, nostalgic, gentle, melancholy, pure, cinematic (lo-fi/classic).
- **Key Concepts:** "Celluloid", "Film", "Grain", "Light", "Shadow", "Summer", "Window", "Echo", "Nostalgia".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Fading Film"

- **BPM:** 100-140 BPM.
- **Beat:** Gentle piano backing, subtle acoustic drums, light synth padding. Often sounds like a recorded memory (lo-fi textures).
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Breathant, soft, focusing on lingering vowels and gentle pauses. Feels like a whisper into a warm microphone.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Grainy Memory Framing. Describing feelings through film/camera terms (e.g., "Out of focus", "Overexposed light", "Celluloid noise").
- WEAPON_02: Tactile Nostalgia. Focus on the physical feeling of old objects (e.g., "Dusty book", "Worn-out shoes", "Rough paper").
- WEAPON_03: Soft-Light Synesthesia. Describing sunlight as if it has a sound or a weight (e.g., "Dazzling noise", "Heavy shadow").
- WEAPON_04: Hesitant Particle 'Kana' (かな). Using 'kana' to express a gentle, uncertain wondering (e.g., "Was it real, I wonder?").
- WEAPON_05: Echoing Sentence Structure. Repeating the last word of a line as a soft echo.
- WEAPON_06: Past-Tense 'Ta' (〜た) focus. Ending many lines in the past tense to root the song in memory.
- WEAPON_07: Fragmented Summer Imagery. (e.g., "Cicadas", "Blue sky", "Ice melting", "Scent of rain").
- WEAPON_08: Distant 'Sayonara'. Framing goodbyes as distant, non-painful but melancholic echoes.
- WEAPON_09: Personification of Inanimate Objects. (e.g., "The window is crying", "The piano remembers").
- WEAPON_10: Rhyming through Nasal 'n' sounds.
- WEAPON_11: Rhetorical 'Sotto' (살며시/Softly). Starting actions with "Gently..." or "Quietly...".
- WEAPON_12: Color-Agnostic descriptions. Using light intensity (Shine, Fade) instead of specific colors.
- WEAPON_13: Incomplete 'Te' (〜て) transitions before the chorus.
- WEAPON_14: Metaphor of 'Crossing Lines' (e.g., "Between today and yesterday").
- WEAPON_15: Use of archaic or slightly formal kanji for common words to add a "classic" feel.
- WEAPON_16: Final Fading Line. A line that ends mid-thought or with an ellipsis, as if the memory is cutting out.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

An empty classroom in the afternoon or a quiet park in autumn. The lyrics should be nostalgic, gentle, and sepia-toned. Avoid fast, aggressive, or modern-tech cliches.
