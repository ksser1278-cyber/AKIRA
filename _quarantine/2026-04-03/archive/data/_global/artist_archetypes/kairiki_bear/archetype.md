# Kairiki Bear Songwriting Archetype: Venomous Obsessive Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- Venomous Obsessive Rock: Aggressive, addictive, and obsessive high-energy guitar rock.
- Core engine: Using "internal toxicity" and social "paralysis" as metaphors for interpersonal dysfunction.
- Relentless rhythmic pressure that feels like a poisonous "Bug" burrowing into the listener's ear.
- Persona of the "Infected Patient" who delivers cynical truths through high-mora density stuttering.

## 2. Title Patterns

- pattern: single poisonous noun | strength: high | risk: over-exposure to generic chemical or medical motifs
- pattern: obsessive verb repetition | strength: high | risk: rhythmic monotony if not paired with melodic energy
- pattern: clinical status label | strength: medium | risk: too cold/detached without the rock energy

## 3. Hook Construction

- density: Extremely high. Rapid-fire mora-density that creates a feeling of obsessive urgency.
- ignition: Insistent melodic hook. The title is often preceded by a sharp, "stuttering" syllabic pre-hook.
- repetition: Obsessive stuttering (e.g., Be-be-be-venom) and looping melodic phrases.
- line_length: Compact and driving. Short mora segments designed for high-velocity delivery.
- forbidden: Bright/healthy themes, spacious ballads, organic pop textures, wholesome resolutions.

## 4. Verse Behavior

- role: Internal toxicity buildup and establishment of the "infected" psychological state.
- stance: Poisonous, cynical, and hopelessly addictive. High use of internal monologue.
- pace: Relentless high-speed pulse. The rhythmic grid is crowded with information.
- shift_pattern: Sudden drops from aggressive belting into "cynical whispers" to highlight toxic realizations.

## 5. Bridge Final Chorus Behavior

- bridge_role: Sudden collapse or "paralysis" moment. The rhythm stops or distorts to expose raw disgust.
- final_chorus_role: Final saturation of venom. The maximum expression of the addictive rhythmic loop.
- release_markers: High-velocity stuttering, cynical "~sa" particles, and final distorted guitar screams.

## 6. Common Imagery

- Toxicity/Poison (venom, bug, acid, alkali, infection, liquid, injection)
- Physical paralysis (needle, numbness, stillness, stiffness, unable to move, bandages)
- Synesthetic disgust (sticky love, bitter smile, rotten heart)
- Clinical/Sickroom (medicine, pills, hospitals, dizzy, downer, over-prescribed)
- Obsessive love/hate (kill the love, love the kill, me-me-me self-fixation)
- Neon-lit darkness (gloomy laboratories, messy rooms, screens reflecting fatigue)

## 7. Emotional Arc Types

- Infection -> Rising Toxicity -> Total Paralysis
- Love -> Obsessive Hate -> Shared Destruction
- Social Stigma -> Internal Bug -> Manic Outburst

## 8. Leakage Risks

- title_leakage: Directly cloning "Venom", "Bug", or "Alkali" as the central concept.
- hook_leakage: Verbatim reproduction of the "Be-be-be-venom" stuttering rhythmic skeleton.
- scene_leakage: Generic "medicine/pill" imagery without the specific Kairiki-style rock intensity.
- phrase_leakage: Verbatim use of signature interjections like "Sakebe" or the specific "~sa" cynical endings.

## 9. Safe Originality Zone

- Set the scene in a dimly lit, messy laboratory or a distorted neon-lit sickroom.
- Escalate internal emotional "sickness" into an aggressive, poisonous rock anthem.
- Maintain repetitive, addictive hooks while the lyrics explore themes of social and personal paralysis.
