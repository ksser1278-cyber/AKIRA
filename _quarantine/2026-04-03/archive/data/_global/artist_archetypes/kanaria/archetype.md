# Kanaria Songwriting Archetype: Royal-Minimalist Predation

## 1. Core Identity

- Royal-Minimalist Predation: Emphasizing power games, arrogance, and calculated focus.
- Sleek, modern, and high-budget aesthetic that avoids emotional whining in favor of superior detachment.
- Persona of a "Predator" watching from a throne, utilizing negative space and sharp staccato delivery.
- High-velocity minimal pop that pairs aggressive bass/traps with soaring vocal peaks.

## 2. Title Patterns

- pattern: english royal status | strength: high | risk: generic "king/queen" tropes without gritty specifics
- pattern: short phonetic burst | strength: high | risk: loss of conceptual weight or narrative depth
- pattern: minimalist negation | strength: medium | risk: too repetitive or lacking emotional hook

## 3. Hook Construction

- density: High-speed minimal peaks. The hook is built around a few high-impact slogans.
- ignition: Immediate title drop. Chorus often starts with the central status marker or command.
- repetition: Structural repetition of spatial or status-based anchors as rhythmic drivers.
- line_length: Breathless staccato bursts followed by a single, long-sustained high note.
- forbidden: Emotional whining, domestic pop cliches, cluttered or organic instrumentation.

## 4. Verse Behavior

- role: Power-play observation, setting the predatory stage/mood.
- stance: Superior and watching from above, maintaining cold detachment from the subject.
- pace: Point-blank staccato. Syllables are delivered like short, sharp stabs.
- shift_pattern: Sharp contrast between the "talking" verses and the "soaring" royalty of the chorus.

## 5. Bridge Final Chorus Behavior

- bridge_role: Strategic pause or a deeper predatory growl/breathy whisper before the final kill.
- final_chorus_role: Ultimate throne-room climax, maximizing the "Royal" vocal energy.
- release_markers: High vocal peaks, English status-slogans, and final bass-heavy drops.

## 6. Common Imagery

- Royal status (crowns, thrones, red carpet, gold, king/queen/prince)
- Predator/Prey (lion, bird, bait, trap, glistening teeth, claws)
- Spatial power (left side, right side, above, below, center stage)
- Sharp textures (eyes, diamonds, glass, mirrors, cold light)
- Modern luxury (spotlights, neon-lit voids, high-budget silence)
- Body mechanics (breathing, pulse, stillness before action)

## 7. Emotional Arc Types

- Observation -> Predation -> Royal Consumption
- Boredom -> Intrigue -> Power Play Climax
- Arrogant Silence -> Aggressive Claim -> Absolute Rule

## 8. Leakage Risks

- title_leakage: Directly cloning "KING", "QUEEN", or "EYE" without a new thematic angle.
- hook_leakage: Verbatim reproduction of the "Left side right side" or "13 14" counting structures.
- scene_leakage: Generic ivory-tower observation without the unique "glistening teeth" or "predatory" grit.
- phrase_leakage: Overuse of signature "Ah" or "A-ha" ad-libs used as rhythmic markers.

## 9. Safe Originality Zone

- Utilize the vocabulary of collective power and predation within a sleek, minimalist modern setting.
- Focus on the persona "watching" from a position of strength, using wealth and status as metaphors for emotional dominance.
- Push the boundaries of "Cool" and "Arrogant" while maintaining high-energy electronic momentum.
