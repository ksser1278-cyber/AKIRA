# scop Songwriting Archetype: Emotional Piano-Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Bittersweet daily life, emotional fingerprints, "Finger Bridge", "Tokyo Station", parting, nostalgia, gentle regret.
- **Tone:** Delicate, emotional, piano-driven, melodic, sincere.
- **Key Concepts:** "Yubikiri", "Finger Bridge", "Tokyo Station", "Irony", "Rain", "Goodbye".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Emotional Pulse"

- **BPM:** 120-155 BPM.
- **Beat:** Piano-led emotionally-charged rock, clean drums, expressive basslines.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Melodic, emotive, focus on natural prosody. Emphasizes breathy delivery and emotional peaks.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (scop DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Fragile Daily Details. Describing small, mundane objects with deep emotional weight (e.g., "The pattern on the curtain", "A half-empty coffee mug", "The sound of the station announcement").
- WEAPON_02: Bittersweet Parting Scenarios. Scenario-based lyrics about saying goodbye or being apart (e.g., "Tokyo Station at night", "The last train", "Waving goodbye").
- WEAPON_03: Delicate Physical Promises. Mentioning hands, fingers, or small physical gestures to represent emotional ties (e.g., "Yubikiri" (Pinky promise), "Linking fingers", "The warmth of your hand").
- WEAPON_04: Gentle Social Irony. Noticing the contradiction between the world's indifference and one's own intense feelings.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A rainy afternoon by a window or a quiet train station at dusk. The lyrics should be emotional, sincere, and musically delicate. Avoid aggressive, technical, or overtly abstract industrial metaphors.
