# 40mP Songwriting Archetype: Daily Life Piano-Pop (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Gentle daily life, soft romances, relatable struggles, legal/metaphorical twists (e.g., "Love Trial"), piano-driven melodies, "Ren'ai Saiban".
- **Tone:** Gentle, sincere, melodic, relatable, slightly playful but honest.
- **Key Concepts:** "Trial", "Evidence", "Daily life", "Apology", "Time", "Clock", "Cleaning".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Daily Rhythm"

- **BPM:** 120-155 BPM.
- **Beat:** Piano-led pop-rock, clean acoustic/pop drums, melodic basslines. Often uses light, airy arrangements.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Clear, narrative, focus on natural prosody. Emphasizes "storytelling" beats and emotional sincerity. Moderate syllabic density.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Gentle Narrative Framing. Setting a specific, relatable scene (e.g., "Morning coffee", "Walking to the station", "Cleaning the room").
- WEAPON_02: Legal/Social Metaphors for Love. Using formal or legal terms for personal feelings (e.g., "Guilty", "Evidence", "Trial", "Verdict").
- WEAPON_03: Relatable Daily Conflict. Describing small, mundane misunderstandings (e.g., "A forgotten message", "A slight frown", "Misplaced keys").
- WEAPON_04: Time-Measurement Metaphors. Using clocks, calendars, or seasons to track emotional shifts (e.g., "Six o'clock shadow", "Calendar marks").
- WEAPON_05: Soft Particle 'Nee' (ねぇ). Using 'nee' to invite the listener into a gentle, personal conversation.
- WEAPON_06: Polite-Causal Shift. Mixing 'desu/masu' and causal forms to show shifting emotional distance.
- WEAPON_07: Apologetic 'Gomen' Hooks. Using "I'm sorry" or self-reflection as a recurring thematic hook.
- WEAPON_08: Acoustic Onomatopoeia. Soft sounds (e.g., "Kachi-kachi" for a clock, "Hu-hu" for a breeze).
- WEAPON_09: Domestic Setting Detail. Describing the physical layout of a room to reflect a mood (e.g., "Dust on the shelf", "Light through the curtain").
- WEAPON_10: Rhyming Verb Couples. Using paired verbs that rhyme in their dictionary form (e.g., "Aruku, miraku").
- WEAPON_11: Rhetorical 'Honto wa' (사실은). Starting a confession with "Actually..." or "In truth...".
- WEAPON_12: Comparison with Ordinary Objects. (e.g., "My heart is like an old chair").
- WEAPON_13: Seasonal Color Palette. Using soft colors (Pastels, Sepia) in descriptions.
- WEAPON_14: Melodic Question/Answer structures within a single verse.
- WEAPON_15: Breath-Mark Pacing. Using short ellipses (...) for a hesitant, gentle feeling.
- WEAPON_16: Final Sincere Blessing. Ending with a wish for the other person's happiness.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A sunny kitchen in the morning or a quiet street at sunset. The lyrics should be gentle, relatable, and narratively sincere. Avoid aggressive, loud, or technical-digital cliches.
