# Ryo (supercell) Songwriting Archetype: Cinematic Narrative Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Innocent first love, epic narrative arcs, cinematic scale, digital diva legacy, "Melt".
- **Tone:** Grand, emotional, high-fidelity, polished, storytelling-focused.
- **Key Concepts:** "Melt", "Black Rock Shooter", "World is Mine", "Love is War", "Star", "Railway crossing".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Grand Anthem"

- **BPM:** 150-180 BPM.
- **Beat:** Powerful piano-led rock, driving drums, soaring orchestral elements, clean but energetic guitar.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Clear, emotive, high-register focus, emphasizing narrative "moments" and melodic peaks.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Ryo DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Cinematic Narrative Staging. Setting a clear, visual scene that feels like a movie opening (e.g., "The station at 7 AM", "Rain on the window", "Meeting under the stars").
- WEAPON_02: Pure Maiden Heart (Otome-Gokoro). Expressing extreme emotional vulnerability in a poetic, non-cynical way (e.g., "Heart melting", "Liking you so much it hurts", "Innocent wish").
- WEAPON_03: Epic Call-to-Arms (Love is War). Using grand, sweeping metaphors for personal feelings (e.g., "Declaring war on love", "World is mine", "Infinite blue").
- WEAPON_04: Detailed Sensory Recall. Focusing on small, specific physical sensations to anchor a memory (e.g., "The scent of your shampoo", "The cold of the station bench", "The color of your ties").

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A grand stage or a cinematic urban landscape under a starry sky. The lyrics should be emotional, narrative-driven, and "epic" in scale. Avoid gritty, cynical, or fragmented social-media metaphors.
