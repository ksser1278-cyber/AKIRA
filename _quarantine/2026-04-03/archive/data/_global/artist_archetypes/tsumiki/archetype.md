# Tsumiki Songwriting Archetype: Math-Rock / Electro-Fusion (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Counterfeit identities, social masks, high-density information, "Phony", "Love", "Fake", mathematical precision, "Anomalies", cognitive dissonance.
- **Tone:** Technical, sharp, multi-layered, fast, sophisticated, cynical, emotionally "shattered".
- **Key Concepts:** "Phony", "Fake", "Anomalies", "Mirror", "Logic", "Rhythm", "Counterfeit", "Mask", "Uncanny Valley".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Math-Rock Pulse"

- **BPM:** 160-200 BPM.
- **Beat:** Extremely complex, math-rock influenced drums with polyrhythmic fills. Glitchy electronics layered with sharp distorted guitars.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** High-density syllabic delivery. Frequent use of "skip" motifs (dropping a beat suddenly) and rapid pitch shifting. Focus on technical "word-stacking" and polyphonic layering.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Identity-Mask Dissonance. Describing a 'self' as an artificial construct (e.g., "Phony", "Counterfeit", "Irony of the mirror").
- WEAPON_02: High-Density Information Stacks. Piling up multi-syllabic technical terms in a single rapid phrase (e.g., "Anomalies, logic, synthesis, fake").
- WEAPON_03: Rhythmic Phonetic Shifts. Using wordplay where the sound creates a rhythmic loop (e.g., "Pa-pa-pa-phony", "Tsu-tsu-tsu-tsumiki").
- WEAPON_04: Mathematical Emotional Calculation. Describing feelings through precise geometric terms (e.g., "45-degree angle", "Parallel lines").
- WEAPON_05: Inverted Subject-Verb logic (倒置法). Reversing the natural order of Japanese sentences to create a "glitchy" feeling (e.g., "Sayonara, this world to").
- WEAPON_06: Specific Particle 'Nara' (なら). Using conditional 'nara' to frame emotions as logical scenarios (e.g., "If I were a mirror, then...").
- WEAPON_07: Dual-Meaning Kanji/Katakana. Layering a word with its technical counterpart (e.g., 愛(System), 心(Data)).
- WEAPON_08: Stuttered Consonant Hits. Placing hard 'k', 't', 'p' sounds at the start of lines to match snare hits.
- WEAPON_09: Fragmented Rhetorical Questions. Asking "Who am I?" or "What is this?" in a rapid, cynical manner.
- WEAPON_10: Material Science Metaphors. Describing emotions as textures or materials (e.g., "Plastic heart", "Iron-clay love").
- WEAPON_11: Unresolved Sentence Endings. Ending a phrase with a particle like 'te' (〜て) or 'de' (〜で) to imply a cycle.
- WEAPON_12: Semantic Saturation. Repeating the same word until it sounds like noise/glitch.
- WEAPON_13: Urban Noise Interjections. Using station announcements or city sounds as verbal fragments.
- WEAPON_14: Mirror-Image Phrasing. Constructing lines that physically reflect each other in mora structure (e.g., 7-5-7-5).
- WEAPON_15: Cognitive Dissonance Vocabulary. Words like "Contradiction", "Error", "Fake", "Real" used interchangeably.
- WEAPON_16: Final-Line "System Shutdown". Ending with a cold, logical statement that cuts through the musical noise.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A digital glitch-space or a sophisticated, neon-lit urban alleyway. The lyrics should be fast, technical, and intellectually dense. Avoid slow, simple, or purely domestic-relatable themes.
