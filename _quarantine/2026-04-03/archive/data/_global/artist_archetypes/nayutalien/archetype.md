# NayutalieN Songwriting Archetype: Alien Space-Pop-Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Extraterrestrial life, space travel, alien romance, solar systems, catchy repetition, "Alien Alien".
- **Tone:** Upbeat, mysterious, catchy, simple but effective, adventurous.
- **Key Concepts:** "Alien", "Space", "Solar System", "Disco", "Rocket", "UFO", "Communication".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Cosmic Pulse"

- **BPM:** 150-180 BPM.
- **Beat:** Driving rock with synth-pop elements. Steady, catchy drum patterns. High rhythmic clarity.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Highly repetitive and melodic. Focus on catchy earworm hooks and "call-and-response" patterns.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (NayutalieN DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Extraterrestrial Interjections. Using space-related terminology as rhythmic markers (e.g., "Alien", "Solar", "UFO", "Roger").
- WEAPON_02: Simple Repetitive Affirmations. Repeating short, positive or catchy phrases to build an earworm (e.g., "Alien alien", "Solar system disco", "I love you").
- WEAPON_03: Cosmic Distance Narratives. Describing human emotions through vast space distances (e.g., "Millions of light years away", "Across the galaxy", "From the other planet").
- WEAPON_04: Technical Comm-Link Metaphors. Describing love as a signal or a communication attempt (e.g., "Radio waves", "Check roger", "Signal received").

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A retro-futuristic spaceship or a moonlit landscape under a giant Saturn. The lyrics should be catchy, simple, and space-themed. Avoid dark, gritty, or overly complex literary abstractions.
