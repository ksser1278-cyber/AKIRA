# Kemu Songwriting Archetype: Theatrical High-Velocity Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Supernatural powers, tragic legends, societal chaos, "Life Reset Button", theatrical storytelling, "Invisible".
- **Tone:** Aggressive, fast, dramatic, heroic but tragic.
- **Key Concepts:** "Kami-sama", "Reset", "Invisible", "Six Trillion Years", "Deceitful", "Miracle".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Panic Theater"

- **BPM:** 180-220 BPM.
- **Beat:** Driving rock drums, aggressive slap bass, orchestral hits, rapid-fire guitar riffs.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Breathless, high-pitched, explosive. Emphasizes "shouted" melodic phrases and dramatic pauses.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Kemu DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Supernatural Tragedy Narratives. Describing a character with a "god-like" or "impossible" burden (e.g., "The boy with the reset button", "The girl who became invisible", "Six trillion years of waiting").
- WEAPON_02: Aggressive Social Negation. Rejecting the world or the system in a dramatic, theatrical way (e.g., "Bye-bye world", "Reset everything", "I don't need miracles").
- WEAPON_03: Theatrical Interjections. Using grand, cinematic words as structural markers (e.g., "Ladies and Gentlemen", "Checkmate", "Game Over").
- WEAPON_04: High-Pressure Emotional Stacking. Piling up short, intense verbs to describe a mental spiral (e.g., "Sobbing, screaming, breaking, vanishing").

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A destroyed city under a red sky or a grand, decaying theater. The lyrics should be fast, dramatic, and narratively dense. Avoid slow, quiet, or purely domestic-relatable themes.
