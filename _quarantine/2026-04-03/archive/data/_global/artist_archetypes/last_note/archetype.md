# Last Note. Songwriting Archetype: High-Velocity High-School Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** High-school youth, "Setsuna" (Momentary) escapes, lighthearted rebellion, energetic daydreaming, "Mikagura School Suite".
- **Tone:** Upbeat, bright, fast, catchy, refreshing.
- **Key Concepts:** "Setsuna Trip", "Mikagura", "Moment", "Daydream", "Escape", "Guitar".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Accelerating Daydream"

- **BPM:** 185-215 BPM.
- **Beat:** Driving guitar rock, bright piano dekorations, fast and steady drums. High energy throughout.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Energetic, rhythmic, using catchy "scat"-like syllables (e.g., "Ah-ha", "Let's go"). Focus on rapid-fire but cheerful delivery.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Last Note. DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Momentary Escape Directives. Asking to "trip" or "escape" from reality into a daydream (e.g., "Setsuna trip", "Diving into the melody", "Bye-bye reality").
- WEAPON_02: High-School Energy Burst. Using vocabulary related to sports, club activities, or energetic youth (e.g., "Full speed", "Dashing through", "Unstoppable").
- WEAPON_03: Playful Self-Correction. Using conversational interruptions to correct one's own thoughts (e.g., "Wait, that's not it", "Just kidding", "Maybe so").
- WEAPON_04: Sonic Onomatopoeia. Using rhythmic sounds to match the guitar riffs (e.g., "Jan-jan", "Paka-paka", "Boom!").

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A bright school hallway or a sunny rooftop at noon. The lyrics should be fast, cheerful, and full of youthful energy. Avoid dark, gritty, or overly abstract philosophical musings.
