# Maretu Songwriting Archetype: Chiptune-Metal Fusion

## 1. Core Identity

- Oppressive, heavy, chiptune-metal fusion focusing on deep psychological trauma and severe social cruelty.
- Core engine: The track must feel rigidly unyielding, mechanical, and threatening. Love is indistinguishable from violence.
- Persona of "Cold Judgment" that uses formal Japanese to deliver horrifying interpersonal revelations.
- Relentless rhythmic assault where melodies are secondary to percussive, punishing stabs of information.

## 2. Title Patterns

- pattern: dark social phenomenon + noun | strength: high | risk: grounds the song in taboo without sufficient abstraction
- pattern: clinical kanji compounds | strength: high | risk: emotionally detached aesthetic that can feel too "cold"
- pattern: short phonetic accusation | strength: medium | risk: too repetitive or lacking conceptual weight

## 3. Hook Construction

- density: Rhythmic and punishing. The hook hits like a hammer blow rather than providing a soaring melodic release.
- ignition: Accusatory drop. A heavy beat shift accompanied by a mockingly polite or aggressive title drop.
- repetition: Structural rhythmic stabs. Twisting common idioms into violent threats through repetition.
- line_length: Tight 4-syllable or 5-syllable percussive blocks that repeat with slight, uncanny variation.
- forbidden: Soaring melodic vowels, traditional pop "hope", redemption arcs, soothing textures.

## 4. Verse Behavior

- role: Aggressive accusation, social judgment, or clinical recounting of a traumatic event.
- stance: Cold detachment that occasionally boils over into manic, rhythmic rage.
- pace: Heavy, rigid, and unyielding. The mora structure is boxed into a strict mechanical grid.
- shift_pattern: Extreme dissonance created by mixing formal Japanese (Keigo) with visceral, gore-adjacent imagery.

## 5. Bridge Final Chorus Behavior

- bridge_role: Complete chaotic descent. Sudden discord, tempo shifts, or a creepy acoustic void.
- final_chorus_role: Violent release. The peak expression of rage or trauma, showing no possible redemption.
- release_markers: Discordant piano stabs, distorted screams, and final bit-crushed electronic noise.

## 6. Common Imagery

- Abandonment/isolation (locked doors, dark rooms, forgotten toys, coin lockers)
- Social judgment (staring eyes, whispers, branding irons, pointing fingers, collective gossip)
- Physical trauma (scars, clinical hospitals, cutting, choking, lack of oxygen)
- Mechanical urban spaces (concrete, rust, breaking chains, rusted metal, coins falling)
- Bodily breakdown (scab, rotted brain, tearing skin, blood flowing in circuits)
- Distorted childhood (broken playgrounds, failed lessons, childhood trauma echoes)

## 7. Emotional Arc Types

- Cold Keigo -> Rising Anger -> Chaotic Violent Release
- Societal Pressure -> Internal Fracture -> Destructive Lash Out
- False Politeness -> Mocking Revelation -> Absolute Judgment

## 8. Leakage Risks

- title_leakage: Directly cloning "Mind Brand", "Coin Locker Baby", or "Brain Revolution Girl" naming structures.
- hook_leakage: Verbatim reproduction of the rhythmic "Pain, pain, pain" or specific bit-crushed transitions.
- scene_leakage: Excessive "edgelord" violence without the underlying MARETU social/interpersonal critique.
- phrase_leakage: Verbatim use of signature insults like "Bakame" or "Ussee" in a MARETU-specific context.

## 9. Safe Originality Zone

- Abstract psychological horror exploring toxic interpersonal dynamics through a mechanical lens.
- Use of polite emotional distance (Keigo) and sterile metaphors to describe extremely damaging human behavior.
- Maintain a heavy, rigid, chiptune-metal rhythm while delivering clinical revelations of the "dark side" of the self.
