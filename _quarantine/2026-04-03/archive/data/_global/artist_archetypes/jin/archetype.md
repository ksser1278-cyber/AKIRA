# Jin Songwriting Archetype: Narrative Summery Rock (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** "Kagerou Project", narrative rock, "Kagerou Daze", summery yet tragic urban heat, storytelling through music, "Lost Time Memory", aggressive guitar-rock, recurring loops, red eye/powers, teenage rebellion.
- **Tone:** Summery, tragic, aggressive, narrative, high-energy, nostalgic, hot (heat-haze).
- **Key Concepts:** "Kagerou", "Daze", "August 15th", "Heat", "Red", "Eyes", "Loop", "Story", "City", "Summer".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Heat-Haze Groove"

- **BPM:** 150-185 BPM.
- **Beat:** Aggressive and driving guitar-rock drums, sharp and rhythmic basslines, high-velocity guitar riffs with distinct "summery" brightness. High emphasis on narrative momentum and "shimmering" heat textures.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Sharp, energetic, focusing on storytelling and rhythmic "shouting" for emotional peaks. Often features rapid delivery of narrative details, sudden rhythmic pauses for dramatic effect, and a "young" but aggressive vocal tone.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Summery Heat-Haze Framing. Starting lines with the physical feeling of August heat (e.g., "The asphalt is shimmering", "Sunlight piercing the eyes").
- WEAPON_02: Narrative Loop Interjections. (e.g., "And it repeats again", "This is the [X]th time").
- WEAPON_03: Red-Eye/Supernatural Metaphors. (e.g., "Eyes turning red", "Deceiving reality", "Stealing the gaze").
- WEAPON_04: High-Velocity Urban Rebellion. (e.g., "Running through the alleyways", "Kicking the world").
- WEAPON_05: Specific Particle 'Sa' (〜さ) for Gritty Narrative Detachment. (e.g., "That's just how the story goes, y'know?").
- WEAPON_06: Rhetorical 'Nani mo' (nothing) as a recurring void motif within the loop.
- WEAPON_07: Staccato Narrative Nouns. (e.g., "Heat, cat, asphalt, blood, red").
- WEAPON_08: Mirror-Phrasing of the Loop. (e.g., "Yesterday was today, today is tomorrow").
- WEAPON_09: Personification of the Heat/Summer. (e.g., "The sun is mocking our struggle").
- WEAPON_10: Rhyming through Sharp, Bouncy Vowels (a, e, o).
- WEAPON_11: Use of 'Kimi' (君) as a partner in a tragic loop or a lost memory.
- WEAPON_12: Metaphor of 'The Stage/The Project' (e.g., "Scripted summer", "Breaking the project").
- WEAPON_13: Incomplete 'Te' (〜て) transitions for a sense of frantic, repeating action.
- WEAPON_14: Rhythmic Stuttering on Key Narrative Verbs (e.g., "Kurikaesu, kurikaesu" / Repeating, repeating).
- WEAPON_15: Use of Katakana for 'Narrative/Project' terms to add a conceptual flair (e.g., "Daze", "Project", "Member").
- WEAPON_16: Final Breaking-the-Loop Line. A line that signals a desperate attempt to break the repetition or a final, tragic acceptance.

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A high-temperature urban street in August or a distorted, repeating summer afternoon. The lyrics should be narrative-driven, aggressive, and summery. Avoid generic love-pop, slow acoustic themes, or purely technical/cold cliches.
