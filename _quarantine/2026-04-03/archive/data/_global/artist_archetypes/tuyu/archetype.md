# TUYU Songwriting Archetype: High-Velocity Jazz-Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Self-deprecation, social anxiety, rapid emotional shifts, urban dark-pop, comparing oneself to others, "Compared Child".
- **Tone:** Aggressive, fast, technically complex, emotionally raw, cynical.
- **Key Concepts:** "Compared Child", "Under Kids", "It's Carnival", "Stop-Self", "Blue", "Rain".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Panic Groove"

- **BPM:** 170-220 BPM.
- **Beat:** Extremely fast jazz-fusion guitar riffs, complex drum fills, rapid piano staccatos. Polyrhythmic elements common.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Rapid-fire (machine gun style) delivery, sudden pitch jumps, highly syncopated and rhythmic.

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (TUYU DNA)

- WEAPON_01: Self-Deprecating Machine Gun. Stacking multiple negative self-comparisons in a single rapid phrase (e.g., "I'm trash", "I'm useless", "Compared to them...").
- WEAPON_02: Rhythmic Negation. Using rapid "No/Stop" interjections to break the melodic flow (e.g., "Dare mo inai", "Yamechatte", "Sonna koto nai").
- WEAPON_03: Rain-Slickened Urban Loneliness. Describing the city as a cold, rainy place where one is "Under Kids" (e.g., "Slippery street", "Puddle of tears", "Station platform").
- WEAPON_04: Sarcastic Carnival. Using festive or upbeat musical terms to describe a personal tragedy or spiral (e.g., "Enjoy the end", "It's a carnival of pain", "Dance in the rain").

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

A rainy city street at 3 AM or a crowded train station where everyone feels like a stranger. The lyrics should be fast, technical, and emotionally intense. Avoid slow, gentle, or purely positive "healing" imagery.
