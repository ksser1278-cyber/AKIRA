# Eve Songwriting Archetype: Cinematic Dark-Fantasy Pop (HYPER-DENSITY)

## 1. Core Identity

- **Thematic Core:** Theatricality, "Dramaturgy", social masks, internal monsters, cinematic dark-fantasy, "Asura-chan", sophisticated introspection, urban surrealism.
- **Tone:** Cinematic, theatrical, sharp, slightly dark, sophisticated, catchy but deep.
- **Key Concepts:** "Drama", "Mask", "Monster", "Blue", "Dramaturgy", "Theatre", "Play", "Actor", "Anomalies", "Demon".

## 2. Rhythmic DNA: The "Theatrical Groove"

- **BPM:** 135-165 BPM.
- **Beat:** Sophisticated guitar-pop drums, melodic and driving basslines, clean yet layered guitar textures. Often features theatrical "stops" and "curtain-call" melodic swells.
- **Vocal Phrasing:** Sharp, rhythmic, focusing on sophisticated wordplay and the "masking" of emotions. Features iconic "Eve-style" high-pitched accents and rhythmic "grunts" or "breaths".

## 3. Linguistic Fingerprint Pool (Hyper-Density: 16 WEAPONS)

- WEAPON_01: Theatrical Meta-Framing. Describing life as a stage, play, or script (e.g., "Dramaturgy", "Curtain call", "Scripted love").
- WEAPON_02: Internal Monster Imagery. Describing insecurities as physical monsters or demons (e.g., "Demon in the closet", "Eating the heart").
- WEAPON_03: Fragmented Blue Solitude. Using "Blue" or "Aqua" as a metaphor for deep, sophisticated loneliness.
- WEAPON_04: Conversational Masking. Using logic or "Adult-like" phrases to hide a child's fear.
- WEAPON_05: Specific Particle 'Sa' (〜さ) for Theatrical Detachment. Creating a sense of a narrator observing a play.
- WEAPON_06: Rhetorical 'Hontou no' (진짜의/True). Repeatedly asking "What is the true [X]?"
- WEAPON_07: Staccato Identity Nouns. (e.g., "Mask, actor, stage, shadow").
- WEAPON_08: Mirror-Phrasing of Dissonance. (e.g., "Smiling while crying", "Living while dying").
- WEAPON_09: Personification of the Script. (e.g., "The words are moving on their own").
- WEAPON_10: Rhyming through Sophisticated Kanji/Katakana blends.
- WEAPON_11: Use of 'Boku' (僕) as a fragile protagonist in a grand theater.
- WEAPON_12: Metaphor of 'Invisible Threads' (e.g., "Pulled by a wire", "Marionette logic").
- WEAPON_13: Incomplete 'Te' (〜て) / 'Nagara' (〜ながら) transitions to imply ongoing performance.
- WEAPON_14: Rhythmic Stuttering on Theatrical Terms (e.g., "D-d-drama").
- WEAPON_15: Use of French/English 'Stage' terminology for sophisticated flair.
- WEAPON_16: Final Curtain-Call Line. A line that ends with a bow or a final, irreversible "The End".

## 4. Safe Originality Zone

An abandoned theater at midnight or a rainy, neon-lit cityscape with fantasy elements. The lyrics should be cinematic, theatrical, and sophisticated. Avoid generic school-life pop or overly bright/simple themes.
