# Neru Songwriting Archetype: Self-Destructive Rock

## 1. Core Identity

- Self-Destructive Rock: Expressing self-mutilation (metaphorical), social rejection, and raw rage against adulthood or school.
- Core engine: Using aggressive, desperate, and explosive punk/metal energy to articulate the internal collapse of urban youth.
- Persona of the "Delinquent Philosopher" who questions authority through surgical and industrial metaphors.
- High-velocity delivery that often peaks in emotional screams and percussive, negative English commands.

## 2. Title Patterns

- pattern: surgical/industrial tool | strength: high | risk: over-exposure to generic hardware or medical motifs (e.g., Scissors)
- pattern: absolute solution/logic | strength: high | risk: appearing too cold or detached from the raw emotional core
- pattern: defiant self-address | strength: medium | risk: slipping into predictable or generic teenage angst

## 3. Hook Construction

- density: Violent and breathless. The hook is packed with high-velocity emotional pressure and informational density.
- ignition: Immediate explosion. The chorus starts with a percussive command or a titular shout (e.g., "Shut Up!", "Tokyo").
- repetition: Manic repetition of "Negative Omnipotence" claims (e.g., Zenchi Zennou / All-knowing and All-powerful).
- line_length: Short, sharp, peak-focused phrasing that mimics the act of "cutting" through the beat.
- forbidden: Gentle piano ballads without a dark twist, wholesome growth arcs, soaring romantic vowels.

## 4. Verse Behavior

- role: Surgical identity remake or clinical recounting of social wounds and "rejection" notices.
- stance: Aggressed, desperate, and raw. The narrator addresses authority (teachers, parents) with sarcastic questions.
- pace: Cutting edge punk/metal. The mora structure is driven by high-speed drum patterns.
- shift_pattern: Transitions between bursts of violent energy and low, rhythmic sections of resentful mumbles.

## 5. Bridge / Final Chorus Behavior

- bridge_role: Total existential collapse. Questioning one's origin ("Who made me this way?") in a moment of acoustic void.
- final_chorus_role: Ultimate self-destructive explosion. The message is "carved" into the track via maximum volume and speed.
- release_markers: Vocal screams, high-speed drum rolls, and final abrasive industrial noise textures.

## 6. Common Imagery

- Surgical tools (scissors, needles, thread, face cutting, stitching, anesthesia, bandages)
- Industrial/Urban (concrete, rust, scrap metal, electric wires, metal cells, abandoned stations)
- Anti-Authoritarian (teacher/sensei, school desk, uniform, classroom rules, blackboards)
- Negative Omnipotence (Zenchi Zennou, god-like status reached only through total internal failure)
- Social Rejection (garbage, trash, waste, anonymous, stray dog, discarded heart)
- Mechanical failure (short circuit, battery death, broken screens, software errors)
- Violent transformation (carving identity, throwing away the old self, replacing the brain)

## 7. Emotional Arc Types

- Rejection -> Self-Remake -> Explosive Outburst
- Obedience -> Sudden Fracture -> Industrial Rage
- Questioning -> Despair -> Defiant Collapse

## 8. Leakage Risks

- title_leakage: Directly cloning "Tokyo Teddy Bear", "How-to World Domination", or "Law-evading Rock" title structures.
- hook_leakage: Verbatim reproduction of the "Zenchi Zennou" or "Bye-bye" rhythmic anchors from previous hits.
- scene_leakage: Generic "school anger" without the specific surgical or industrial metaphors unique to Neru.
- phrase_leakage: Overuse of "Sensei" or "Shut up" as rhetorical crutches to signal the "Neru" style.

## 9. Safe Originality Zone

- Capture the raw, abrasive frustration of urban youth through a lens of industrial and surgical transformation.
- Utilize the vocabulary of industrial "scrap" and clinical "repair" to describe emotional states.
- Maintain high-speed, hard-hitting punk/metal momentum while the lyrics "carve" out a message of defiance.
