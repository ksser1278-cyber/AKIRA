# Iyowa Songwriting Archetype: Eccentric Dissonant Pop

## 1. Core Identity

- Eccentric Dissonant Piano-Pop: Focusing on abnormality, internalized anxiety, and "Kyu-kurarin" style surrealism.
- Core engine: Pairing beautiful, catchy melodies with intentional dissonance and rhythmic instability.
- Persona of the "Deeply Internalized Anxious Dreamer" who observes mundane life through a distorted, surreal lens.
- Theatrical and expressive delivery that features sudden pitch shifts and informational "overflow".

## 2. Title Patterns

- pattern: onomatopoeic emotional burst | strength: high | risk: over-exposure to specific cute/creepy sound motifs (e.g., Kyu-kurarin)
- pattern: surreal mundane noun | strength: high | risk: loss of narrative grounding or becoming too abstract
- pattern: abstract internal state | strength: medium | risk: too poetic or vauge for structural song branding

## 3. Hook Construction

- density: Expressive and overflowing. The chorus is information-dense but remains melodically engaging.
- ignition: Sudden rhythmic "gasps" or dissonant melodic leaps as the hook starts.
- repetition: Expressive stuttering on key emotional verbs (e.g., Warau, warau / Laughing, laughing) to show instability.
- line_length: Unpredictable and expressive, often hitting sharp "accents" that disrupt standard phrasing.
- forbidden: Simple stable pop rhythms, wholesome cliches, traditional resolved harmonies, slow domesticity.

## 4. Verse Behavior

- role: Establishment of the "Abnormal Everyday" where mundane details slowly twist into surrealism.
- stance: Eccentric, anxious, and deeply internalized. High use of self-address or detached observation.
- pace: Frantic but fragile piano-driven movement. The rhythm feels like it's "running out of breath".
- shift_pattern: Sudden transitions between off-kilter grooves and moments of total, beautiful melodic clarity.

## 5. Bridge Final Chorus Behavior

- bridge_role: Dramatic perspective shift or total internal breakdown into pure, beautiful dissonant layers.
- final_chorus_role: Total dissolution of the self. The peak of the song is a beautiful, dissonant chordal climax.
- release_markers: Piano glissandos, pitch modulation, and sharp, gasping vocal pauses for emotional emphasis.

## 6. Common Imagery

- Organic-Mechanical fusion (circuit toast, oil veins, heart valves, screw coughing, wires in hair)
- Surreal everyday (tilted floors, whispering ceilings, toast as a circuit board, melting snacks)
- Internalized anxiety (heartbeat pulse, error screens, shadows on the wall, heavy breathing)
- Melting/Breaking (melting safety valves, breaking safety lines, overflow of data, dissolution)
- Childlike/Distorted items (ribbons, crayons, broken toys, sweets, childhood memories as glitches)
- Clinical abnormality (data logs, clinical katakana for sensations, weird physical feelings)

## 7. Emotional Arc Types

- Mundane -> Surreal Decay -> Dissonant Break
- Anxiety -> Manic Joy -> Total Dissolution
- Observation -> Internal Distortion -> Beautiful Collapse

## 8. Leakage Risks

- title_leakage: Directly cloning "Kyu-kurarin" or "Heads Up" style puns or onomatopoeia.
- hook_leakage: Verbatim reproduction of the eccentric "off-kilter" syncopation from previous hits.
- scene_leakage: Literal "melting" or "mechanical" scenes without the specific Iyowa-style piano-pop texture.
- phrase_leakage: Verbatim use of "Hontou wa" (사실은) as a rhetorical questioning device too frequently.

## 9. Safe Originality Zone

- Set the scene in a cluttered room at 4 AM or a surreal, slightly tilted version of a city street.
- Utilize the vocabulary of "beautiful discord" and internalized anxiety with a high-energy piano-pop backdrop.
- Maintain a persona that is expressive yet deeply abnormal, avoiding stable or traditionally wholesome pop cliches.
